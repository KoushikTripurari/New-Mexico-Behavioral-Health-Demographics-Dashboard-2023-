New Mexico Behavioral Health & Demographics Dashboard (2023)

1) Population by Race/Ethnicity

  • White residents are the majority across most counties, with notable AI/AN concentrations in McKinley, Cibola, and Rio Arriba.
  • Santa Fe and Bernalillo show greater racial diversity than many rural counties.

2) Age & Gender Distribution

  • Most counties have a clear majority 18+ (75–85%), indicating adult-driven service demand.
  • Younger structures in Lea and McKinley may signal higher future service needs.

3) Service Demand & Behavioral Health Metrics

  • Crisis calls, dispatches, and ER visits are heavily concentrated in Bernalillo, Doña Ana, and San Juan.
  • Smaller rural counties like Catron, De Baca, and Harding show minimal use and low reported deaths.

4) Drug Overdose Deaths (Counts)

  • Bernalillo (376 deaths) accounts for nearly 40% of statewide drug deaths (948 total).
  • Other major contributors include Santa Fe, Doña Ana, and Rio Arriba.

5) Deaths vs Population Context

  • Rio Arriba and Torrance show disproportionately high overdose burdens.
  • Rural counties face outsized mortality despite smaller populations.

6) DWI Case Dispositions

  • Conviction rates average 54% statewide; Rio Arriba and Santa Fe show longer case times and higher pending rates.
  • Faster conviction times in counties like Curry and Guadalupe.

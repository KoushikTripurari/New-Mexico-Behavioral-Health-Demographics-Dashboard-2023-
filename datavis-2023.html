New Mexico Complex Discharges Data Collection

Welcome! Thank you for participating in the UNM Center for Health Policy’s Complex Discharge Study. Below you’ll find instructions for submitting your facility’s data. Data collection will run from January 15 through April 15, 2026.

1) Designate a Point of Contact

Each hospital must identify:

  • Primary point of contact (POC) – responsible for entering and submitting data.
  • Optional secondary POC – may submit on behalf of the primary.

2) Return Your Letter of Support

  • You will receive a Letter of Support (LOS) template from the UNM CHP team.
  • Please return the letter on your facility’s letterhead, signed by the CEO or authorized designee, in PDF format no later than January 5, 2026.
  • You will be unable to submit data if we do not receive the completed letter.
Please email POC information and completed Letters of Support to Stefany Goradia (sngoradia@salud.unm.edu).

3) Track Your Complex Discharge Cases

We recommend maintaining an internal tracking sheet throughout the study period to simplify reporting. Include patients who meet all the following criteria:

  • Population: Inpatient admissions, all insurance types.
  • Avoidable delay: Patient is medically ready but experiencing a discharge delay.
  • Discharge date: falls between January 15 – April 15, 2026.
  • Exclusion: Do not include patients in swing bed status.

4) Data Submission Guidelines

  • Submit cases as they occur, or in batches (i.e., weekly).
  • At minimum, please submit at least once per month (by the last day of February, March, and April).
  • One survey submission = one delayed discharge case.
  • Submit data only after the patient has been discharged.
  • If a patient remains admitted as of April 15, the survey includes a field to indicate this.

How to Submit Data

  • Click on the REDCap survey link (use the same link to submit data throughout the study period)
  • Review the consent information.
  • If you agree, enter the unique hospital identifier provided to you by the UNM CHP team.
  • Complete all required fields and click Submit.
  • Repeat for each complex patient discharge being reported.
  • Repeat this process for each patient meeting criteria.

Please note that you will be logged out after 30 minutes of inactivity; incomplete submissions will appear as highlighted.

Working definitions

Complex discharge

When a patient is stabilized and medically ready for discharge, but is unable to be discharged due to internal or external barriers.

Total avoidable days

The total number of days the patient remained delayed in the hospital after being medically ready for discharge. If discharge readiness happened partway through a day, count it as one full day.

Insurance/financial coverage days

Days waiting for an insurer to make a decision (e.g., prior authorization, continued-stay review, financial eligibility determination). Includes all days waiting on insurer response.

Placement setting/screening days

Days spent waiting for a facility or service (e.g., SNF, assisted living, group home, rehab, behavioral health facility, home care agency) to review the referral, accept the patient, and confirm a bed is available.

Agency process days

Days spent waiting for state agency involvement, including eligibility determinations, assignment of workers, required assessments, or decisions about accepting the referral (e.g., CYFD, APS, BHSD, DDSD, ISD). Medical insurance eligibility should be captured under the "Insurance or financial coverage" category.

Guardianship days

Days waiting for a guardian or legal decision-maker to be appointed when none is available, from initiation of guardianship proceedings to legal finalization that enables discharge decisions. Includes time required for court processes, attorney coordination, and required evaluations.

Advance care planning days

Days spent completing required documentation or decision-making related to goals of care (e.g., advance directives, healthcare proxy, POST/MOLST forms) that must be finalized before safe discharge or placement.

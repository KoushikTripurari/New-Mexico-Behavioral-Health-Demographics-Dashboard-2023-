New Mexico Complex Discharges Data Collection

Welcome! Thank you for participating in the UNM Center for Health Policy’s Complex Discharge Study. Below you’ll find instructions for submitting your facility’s data. Data collection will run from January 15 through April 15, 2026.

1) Designate a point of contact

  • This person will be responsible for submitting data.
  • You may also designate an optional secondary point of contact.

2) Return a letter of support

  • A template letter will be sent to you.
  • Please return the letter on your facility’s letterhead, signed by the CEO or authorized designee, in PDF format no later than January 5, 2026.
  • You will be unable to submit data if we do not receive the completed letter.

3) Collect your data

Include cases that meet all criteria below; you may optionally include shorter delays.
  • Population: Inpatient admissions
  • Avoidable delay: Anyone experiencing discharge delays
  • Period: Present between January 15 – April 15
  • Exclusion: Not in a swing bed

4) Submit your data

  • You will receive a REDCap survey link.
  • Use the same link to submit data throughout the entire study period.
  • Report cases as they occur.
  • You will submit a survey response for each patient with a delayed, complex discharge.
  • Data should be submitted at minimum monthly (by the end of the month for February, March, and April).
  • Submit a survey response upon the patient’s discharge. If the patient is still admitted on April 15, please indicate this on the survey.

How to submit a survey

  • Click on the REDCap survey link
  • Read the consent
  • Enter your unique hospital ID number
  • Complete all required fields
  • Click submit

You’ll be logged out after 15 minutes of inactivity; incomplete cases appear highlighted on the homepage.

Working definitions

Complex discharge

When a patient is stabilized and medically ready for discharge, but is unable to be discharged due to internal or external barriers.

Avoidable hours/days

Hours/days when a patient is stabilized and ready for discharge but is unable to be discharged.

Insurance/financial coverage days

From first coverage request to final determination (approval or denial), including insurer correspondence delays.

Placement setting days

From first placement request to notice of availability (bed or slot). Track separate attempts if done in parallel.

Placement screening days

From screening start to screening determination; include back-and-forth for additional information.

Agency eligibility process days

From initial eligibility request to final eligibility determination by the agency.

Agency referral process days

From first referral submission to final referral decision (accept, decline, or waitlist).

Guardianship days

From initiation of guardianship proceedings to legal finalization that enables discharge decisions.

Advance care planning days

From start of advance care planning to completion (for example, health care proxy, MOLST/POST forms).
